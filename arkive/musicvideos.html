ULCERMAN

Released: May 30, 2019

Producer: Jakob Utter

Cameraman: Nathan Rossiter

     ULCERMAN is the nonsensical crap talk anthem of Summer '19. I released the music video on my last day of middle school and somehow I had people saying it was fire? The real treat is the music video, which features me playing with a bird carcass, drinking rainwater, and defacing park property. The instrumental samples The Ink Spots, one of my favorite music groups of all time. Is it stupid? Yes. Is it in your face and over the top? Yes. Is it ULCERMAN? It might just be so!


KING OF THE HILL


Released: October 31, 2019

Producer: flare

Cameraman: Casper XO/Capn' Corrosive

     If anything is to be taken from this song, it's that I'm really immature. But at the same time, see the inner beauty of such an amateur piece of work! KING OF THE HILL represents a turning point for me; as an artist and even as a person. Do you know any other 14 year olds that could've made this song? I didn't think so. This is the track that inspired me to buy a $130 microphone on the very first day of freshman year, I had this track knocked out in like half an hour. It's my biggest song to date with a thousand views on YouTube. That really says something, like I'm just getting started and all that.



I KEEP MY HANDS SANITIZED


Released: May 31, 2021

Producer: Jakob Utter

Cameraman: Lil Lemon/Capn' Corrosive

     What's not to love about the foul-mouthed and infamous "I KEEP MY HANDS SANITIZED"? This is a song for the youth, the people, the world. Breathe it in, embrace it, because this is what real music sounds like. An unbridled creative expression that all music pales into comparison to; an ode to death, hatred, and all things sick and twisted. It wasn't even my intent to have such a lazy throwaway song blow up in my community, but once the people in my town heard the "f***ed a preschooler just to start her off early" lyric, it spread like COVID-19. Things got so bad that some dickwads called the police to complain about these lyrics. Could you imagine that phone call? I didn't get in trouble, the video stayed up and garnered over 200 views in the span of a couple days. So call the cops, spread the word. Do whatever it takes to get me famous.
